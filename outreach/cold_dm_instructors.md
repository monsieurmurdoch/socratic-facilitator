# Cold DM playbook — cohort instructors

## Operating rules

- **Pick one person, write one message.** Templated DMs read templated. Use the structure below; rewrite each time.
- **Lead with something specific you noticed about them**, not about you. Their cohort, a recent post, a teaching choice.
- **One ask. One link. Under 90 words.** Your ask is a 15-min call OR "want to try it free for your next cohort?" — never both.
- **Send between 8–11am their local time, Tue–Thu.** Not Mondays (inbox triage), not Fridays (mental checkout).
- **Reply rate target: 20%+.** If you're under 10% after 30 sends, the message is wrong, not the channel.
- **Track in a spreadsheet.** Name, channel, date sent, reply (y/n), call booked (y/n), signed up (y/n). Cohort-instructor land is small; don't double-DM.

---

## Variant A — flattering observation + specific pain (LinkedIn or Twitter)

> Hi [name] — saw your [specific thing: "Stripe writeup from the last Maven cohort" / "thread on async vs synchronous"]. The bit about [specific quote / takeaway] resonated.
>
> Quick question: when you run a synchronous session with 15+ people, how do you handle the dynamic where the same 3 voices dominate and the quiet ones don't get drawn in? I've been building a tool for exactly that and would love your gut reaction — even if it's "this is dumb."
>
> 3-min Loom: [link]
>
> If it's interesting I'd happily run it free for your next cohort in exchange for honest feedback.

**Why this works:** Specific opener (proves you're real). Names the pain *they* feel without pitching. Loom is optional, not required. Free + honest-feedback ask is low-friction.

---

## Variant B — direct, no Loom (for high-status people whose time is precious)

> Hi [name] — short version: built an AI co-facilitator for live cohort sessions. Surfaces quiet voices, presses unexplored disagreements, writes the post-session debrief. Used in [N] cohorts so far.
>
> Not pitching — looking for one [specific role: instructor / Maven host / Reforge facilitator] to tell me whether the report we generate is actually useful or noise. 15 min, sometime next week?

**Why this works:** Respects their time. Frames you as a peer asking for craft input, not a vendor. Anchored in social proof ("[N] cohorts").

---

## Variant C — the "I built this because I was annoyed" angle (Twitter, casual)

> [name] — your [post / cohort / talk] on [topic] is what got me to actually finish a side thing I'd been sitting on: an AI that quietly co-facilitates multi-person discussions instead of doing the dyadic-tutor thing every other AI tool does.
>
> Would love to put it in front of someone whose taste I trust. 15 min next week, or I can send a 3-min Loom?

**Why this works:** Origin story is honest. "Whose taste I trust" is genuine flattery. Optionality on call vs. Loom respects their preference.

---

## Follow-up (one, never two)

Send 5–7 business days later. Same thread. ≤30 words.

> [name] — bumping in case the original got buried. Totally fine if it's not for you; just didn't want to assume the silence meant no.

**Never send a third message.** Move on. The world is large.

---

## What to do when they reply yes

Send a Calendly link with **two specific time slots already proposed** in the message body. Don't make them pick from a calendar grid. Reduce friction.

> Great. Two slots that work for me next week — pick whichever, or send one back:
> - Tue [date] 10am PT
> - Thu [date] 2pm PT
>
> [Calendly link as fallback]

---

## What to do when they reply "tell me more"

Don't respond with a wall of text. Send the Loom (if you didn't already) and one sentence:

> Easiest is this 3-min walkthrough: [link]. After that, happy to chat or just send you an account to try.

## Channel notes

| Channel | When to use | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn DM | Default for instructors with a public LinkedIn presence | Slightly higher reply rate, more "professional" framing |
| Twitter/X DM | Indie creators, ex-startup people, anyone whose primary platform is Twitter | More casual; reply rate higher if they follow you back first |
| Email (cold) | Last resort; only if you can't find them on LinkedIn/Twitter | Lower reply rate, requires more personalization |
| Maven Slack / Polygence Slack | Soft outreach via being helpful in threads first | Don't pitch in public; DM after 2–3 helpful interactions |

## Target list for the first 50 sends

Build the list before sending anything. Spend a Saturday on it.

- **Maven instructors** (50–100 active English-language ones — sortable by cohort frequency on maven.com)
- **Reforge guest instructors / programs** (~30 active)
- **Section faculty** (~50)
- **Polygence mentors running group tracks** (~20)
- **Skool community owners with paid tiers and live calls** (find via /discovery)
- **Independent cohort-course creators on Twitter** (search bio: "cohort-based course", "live cohort", "next cohort starts")
- **Newsletter creators who run paid live calls** (Lenny's, Every, Steph Smith circle)
- **Ex-On Deck fellows now running their own programs**
