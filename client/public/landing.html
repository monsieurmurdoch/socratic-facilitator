What we run

The seminar experience families usually have to assemble by hand.

Live discussion pods

Small recurring groups meet around a shared text or question. The point is not homework help. It is practice thinking clearly with other people.

Human-led, not chatbot-led

A skilled facilitator holds the room: pacing, warmth, standards, follow-up, and the social confidence young people need to speak.

Custom AI in the room

Plato watches the arc of the conversation, remembers what mattered, and offers timely questions, summaries, and post-session intelligence.

How it works

Built for recurring seminars, not one-off AI demos.

  1. Choose the track.Literature, philosophy, humanities, debate, or a custom sequence for your group.
  2. Gather the pod.Usually 5-8 students with a recurring weekly rhythm and a shared level of seriousness.
  3. Run the room.A human facilitator leads. Plato supports by asking, noticing, remembering, and surfacing the next thread.
  4. Review the trace.Families and facilitators see what happened: transcript, highlights, participation, and next-session notes.
Why it matters

Students need places to practice sustained conversation.

They learn to listen without disappearing.

They learn to disagree without performing.

They learn to build an idea with peers over time.

They learn that intellectual life can be social.
